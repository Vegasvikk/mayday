Arizona Title and EscrowIn Web Design and Strategy/ UXUI / Digital Marketing

Out of the blocks...

Arizona Title and Escrow was one of my first paid projects as a web designer, Flash developer and web strategist. The client wanted a website portal to inform customers about the title and escrow process and to garner advertising from title and escrow companies. This project was finished on time and at a reasonable budget, leaving the client very happy with the results.

  • Client: Tracey Breidt
  • Technology: HTML 4.0, CSS, Flash, Photoshop

The Beginning of Indico Web Development

Even though I was working solo, I decided to form a company which I called Indico, derived from the Latin indico, which means "to publish, to bring forth". I began my freelancing work through this entity and continued with it until 2015, along with some jobs as an regular employee for a local graphics company and a remote position as a web developer for a startup mixed in. I recently decided to move on from freelancing because I miss the interaction between other designers and developers; at this point I feel that a team can bring more to a project in terms of creativity and problem-solving than an individual perspective.

Process of Arizona Title and Escrow Project

  • Interview owner of business, obtain specs, scope, develop business plan
  • Research competitors and determine how they were succeeding and where they were lacking, in order to allow my client to fill that void in the marketplace.
  • Create comps (prototypes) with Photoshop, working very closely with the client in terms of design aesthetics, color scheme, fonts, and information architecture.
  • After signoff on design, create a multi-page website to fulfill the needs of Arizona Title and Escrow.

What I Learned From This Project

Explaining What a Web Domain Can and Cannot Do
Communication

I started this project with an idea that the clients wanted an advertising and information portal, but learned later, well into development, that the backer of the project was not aware that just buying a domain name didn't mean you owned all of that business on the Internet. I had to break the news to the client that they could not corner the market by just buying domain names, but they could still profit from a portal if it was maintained and monetized properly.

A Little Motion in a Website Can Add Interest
Learning new tools

At the time this website was being created, I believe we all were using Flash 4.0. Some designers and developers used it to excess, making buttons blink and making graphics fly out of the page, and this trend in web design is still a concern of mine--don't overuse motion on a website. I used Flash to create a subtle mouseover effect on the buttons, the first time I'd ever used Flash in a website design. But I didn't go overboard. Just because you can make something move, doesn't mean you have to.
